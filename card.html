Axolotl

Axolotls were named after Xolotl, the Aztec god of fire and lightning, who could take on the form of a salamander.

  • Scientific Name: Ambystoma Mexicanum
  • Average Length: 9 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 5-6 years in the wild, 10-15 years in captivity
  • Habitat: Freshwater

The Axolotl is unusual among amphibians because it reaches adulthood without undergoing metamorphosis. They do not heal by scarring, instead they are able to regenerate their limbs, gills and parts of their eyes and brains in a period of months.
